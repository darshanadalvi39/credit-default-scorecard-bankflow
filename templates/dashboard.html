Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is Credit Score?

A Credit score is a 3-digit score highlighting your credit history and rating. Anything between 750 and 900 is considered a good Credit score and helps lenders and financial institutions to decide the associated risk and the creditworthiness of individuals seeking a loan or credit.

2. Is CIBIL score the same as Credit Score?

The terms Credit Score' and 'CIBIL Score' are slightly different. The former is a general term that refers to the credit assessment score given by various credit rating agencies such as Experian, Equifax, Highmark, and others. CIBIL score is specifically provided by CIBIL [Credit Information Bureau (India) Limited].

3. Why should I check my Credit Score?

Faster credit approval: Financial institutions/lenders check your credit score to examine your creditworthiness. A higher Credit Score is associated with a lower risk, accelerating the loan credit/loan approval process.

Lower interest rates: Lenders often offer lower interest rates for individuals with high Credit Scores.

Higher credit limit: If your Credit Score is good, you might be eligible for a higher Credit Card limit or loan amount

Hassle-free rent applications: When you rent a home, your tenant or a property agency may check your Credit Score to understand your financial track record. This may directly impact your chances of successfully renting a home.

4. What is the minimum Credit Score for a loan?

There is no minimum credit score for a loan. However, if you want to ensure that your loan application process is hassle-free, a Credit Score of 750 and above is generally considered good

5. What is a good Credit Score for a Home Loan?

In India, a Credit Score of more than 750 is considered reasonable if you want to apply for a Home Loan. However, some lenders may consider a slightly lower credit score, provided you have a good repayment history.
